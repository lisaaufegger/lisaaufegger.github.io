Context

The Problem

Awareness was strong — but most aware developers weren't building. The largest group, roughly a third, were undecided. The question wasn't how to raise awareness. It was why the platform wasn't converting it.

"Developers aren't just asking 'Can I build this?' — they're asking 'Is it worth building?'"

Research Goals

What I Set Out to Learn

  • 🎯 Identify what drives intent at each funnel stage — and what creates hesitation
  • 📊 Segment developers to find the highest-opportunity conversion cohorts
  • ⚡ Pinpoint product touchpoints most linked to momentum in the first 7 days
  • 🗺️ Produce actionable recommendations with defined success metrics

Approach

Methodology detail
Segmentation, sampling, and analysis
▼

Data quality & preparation. Raw survey exports were consolidated across markets, validated for skip logic and quota compliance, and filtered using pre-specified QC rules — removing speeders, straight-liners, failed attention checks, and duplicates. Four composite indices were constructed (ROI expectations, demand perception, effort-to-start, trusted sources) and validated with reliability checks before combining items.

Segmentation. Two complementary approaches were used: theory-driven rule-based cohorts cut on key indices and funnel stage, and model-assisted clustering on standardised drivers to validate natural groupings. Segments were assessed for size, interpretability, and stability across countries and demographics — then compared on tool adoption, intent, and first-week behaviours to confirm meaningful distinction.

Driver modelling. Three logistic/ordinal regression models were run — Consideration → Intent, Intent → First Build, and Early Activation → Optimisation — with predictors including ROI expectations, demand perception, effort-to-start, trust in sources, and prior experience. Diagnostics included VIF checks for multicollinearity, pseudo R² and AUC for model fit, and sensitivity tests across weighted vs. unweighted samples and alternative codings.

Phase 2 (N≈300). A deeper dive into the first 7 days compared daily vs. non-daily builders and modelled first-week activation using effect sizes (standardised coefficients and odds ratios), segment concentration, and practical feasibility to prioritise the highest-lift levers.

Findings

Three Key Insights

  • 📈
    ROI is the strongest predictor of intent. Developers who saw clear market demand were ~2× more likely to start a project. Messaging was over-indexed on technical features.
  • 🚧
    Setup friction is the biggest barrier. Developers who rated setup as "easy" were ~3× more likely to build in week one. Skill reuse and low-effort porting were the key antidotes.
  • 🎯
    The undecided segment is the largest opportunity. ~35% of aware developers had high curiosity but high friction. Addressing their barriers could unlock the largest conversion gains.
